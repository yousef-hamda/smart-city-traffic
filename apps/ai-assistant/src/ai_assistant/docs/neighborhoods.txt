Jerusalem Neighborhoods — Traffic Profile

1. City Center (מרכז העיר / وسط المدينة):
Historic core. Jaffa Road Light Rail runs through it. High pedestrian volume. Parking very limited. Traffic calmed 30 km/h zone. Peak congestion: 08:00–10:00 and 17:00–20:00.

2. German Colony (המושבה הגרמנית / المستعمرة الألمانية):
Residential. Emek Refaim street is a busy restaurant/cafe strip. Narrow streets. Significant evening traffic Friday–Saturday.

3. Malha (מלחה / ملحة):
Home to Jerusalem's main shopping mall (Malha Mall) and the Biblical Zoo. Major traffic generator on weekends. Connected to Begin Boulevard. Parking structures available.

4. Talpiot (תלפיות / طلبيوت):
Industrial and commercial zone south of the city. Hardware stores, car dealerships. Truck traffic significant. Connected via Derech Hevron.

5. Gilo (גילה / جيلو):
Large residential neighborhood in the south. Main access via Road 60 and Derech Hevron. Population ~40,000. Morning outbound congestion heavy.

6. Har Homa (הר חומה / جبل أبو غنيم):
Newer southern neighborhood. Single main access road. Prone to isolation during incidents. 25,000 residents.

7. French Hill (גבעת הצרפתים / الفرنساوي):
University area, student population. Near Hebrew University main campus on Mount Scopus. Route 1 accessible via the French Hill interchange.

8. Neve Ya'akov (נווה יעקב / نيفي يعكوف):
Northernmost large neighborhood. ~25,000 residents. Connected via Begin Boulevard extension and Route 1 north. Bus lines heavy.

9. Ramot (רמות / راموت):
Northwestern neighborhood. 40,000+ residents. Access primarily via Begin Boulevard (route 9). Severe congestion 07:30–09:30.

10. Pisgat Ze'ev (פסגת זאב / بسجات زئيف):
Large northeastern neighborhood connected to French Hill interchange. 50,000 residents. Jerusalem's largest neighborhood. Tram line planned.
